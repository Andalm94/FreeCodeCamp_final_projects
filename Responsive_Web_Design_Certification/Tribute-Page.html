Julius Caesar

Politician, roman general, conqueror of Gaul

Chronological history

  • 100 b.C.: Born in Rome in the bosom of the gens Julia
  • 60 b.C.: First triumvirate of Caesar, Crassus and Pompey.
  • 58 b.C.: He is appointed proconsul of Gaul. The Gallic War begins.
  • 52 b.C.: Caesar's victory against the Gauls commanded by Vercingetorix.
  • 49 b.C.: The senate declares Caesar an enemy of Rome. Julius Caesar crosses the Rubicon with his troops and marches towards Rome: start of the civil war against Pompey.
  • 46 b.C.: Defeat Pompey and end the Roman civil war. The Senate declares him dictator.
  • 44 b.C.: He was assassinated at the hands of his opponents in the Senate.

If you want to know more about his incredible life, you should read his article in the Encyclopaedia Britannica.
